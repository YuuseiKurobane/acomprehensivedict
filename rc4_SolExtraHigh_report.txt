3. Roman sense numerals on anomalous lexical starts

Reviewed sense sequences are recovered for layak I, mempermak, berseru,
kesoréan, and bertimbalan. This prevents their numbered meanings from being
flattened into the preceding definition text and restores template expansion
under the correct form.

4. False spaces at source-contiguous font boundaries

Layer 1 now emits an explicit [NoSpace] boundary and Layer 2 honors it without
discarding either run's typography. The repair is limited to the 115 audited
source line IDs and still requires touching geometry, no source whitespace,
and a lexical/numeric character boundary.

Exactly 124 audited joins are represented:
  52 suffix/derivational joins
  63 numeric joins
  4 other word joins
  5 mixed SmallCaps/Italic joins

Examples now render as radéns, penghulus, Rp 1.000,-, 11.00, 40.000,
héteroséksual, Saya, Vasnya, IDul, SIRATul-mustakim, KATAnya, UDELnya,
and BELANGnya rather than their falsely spaced rc3 forms.

5. Personal initials and name fragments misread as label badges

Dotted initials/name fragments are protected contextually from source-label
classification. All nine audited false badge conversions in eight examples
are corrected, including Kolonél F. D., sutradara N. A., K. M. Neptune,
Mt. G. Slamet, U. S., Pilot Lim C. L., A., and P. S. Sumlang.

6. Mixed-style arrow cross-references

Layer 1 now preserves the displayed prefix/suffix typography while marking the
SmallCaps lexical target as [SeeBare]. Layer 2 links that target without
printing a second arrow. Adjacent target fragments and reviewed punctuation,
sense, slash-list, and derived-form cases are normalized conservatively.

Results:

  99 unique new target strings are represented.
  All 107 resolve to valid generated destinations.

The expression parser now checks following physical lines for a closing
pronunciation slash, and observed header aliases receive their following span
context. RC4 therefore emits:

and no longer emits their slash-ending rc3 forms.

  no unbalanced lookup parentheses

they are dominated by legitimate homographs/shared derived forms and were not
changed merely to force uniqueness.
